The Odyssey: Character Profiles

Odysseus

Role: Protagonist, King of Ithaca, and a hero of the Trojan War.

Significance: Odysseus is known for his intelligence, cunning, and resilience. His decade-long journey home tests his endurance and leadership. He embodies the Greek ideal of metis (cunning intelligence) and is favored by Athena but opposed by Poseidon.

Penelope

Role: Queen of Ithaca, wife of Odysseus, and mother of Telemachus.

Significance: Penelope is a symbol of loyalty, patience, and cleverness. She fends off the suitors by weaving and unweaving Laertes’ shroud, waiting for Odysseus' return. She also tests him with the bed trick to confirm his identity.

Telemachus

Role: Son of Odysseus and Penelope.

Significance: His journey to find his father is a coming-of-age story where he grows from an inexperienced youth into a capable leader. He helps Odysseus reclaim Ithaca.

Athena

Role: Goddess of wisdom and war, protector of Odysseus.

Significance: Athena guides Odysseus and Telemachus, often disguising herself. She ensures Odysseus’ success and peace in Ithaca.

Poseidon

Role: God of the sea, antagonist to Odysseus.

Significance: Poseidon seeks revenge on Odysseus for blinding his son, Polyphemus, causing Odysseus’ prolonged journey home.

Polyphemus

Role: Cyclops, son of Poseidon.

Significance: Odysseus blinds Polyphemus to escape captivity, invoking Poseidon’s wrath. This act represents intelligence overcoming brute strength.

Calypso

Role: Nymph who detains Odysseus on her island, Ogygia.

Significance: She offers Odysseus immortality, but he longs for home, demonstrating human perseverance and the theme of nostalgia.

Circe

Role: Enchantress who turns Odysseus’ men into pigs.

Significance: She symbolizes temptation and transformation but also aids Odysseus after he resists her magic.

Eumaeus

Role: Loyal swineherd of Odysseus.

Significance: Eumaeus embodies loyalty and xenia (hospitality). He aids Odysseus in reclaiming Ithaca.

Antinous

Role: The most arrogant suitor.

Significance: He leads the suitors in abusing Odysseus’ home and is the first to die when Odysseus takes revenge.

Eurymachus

Role: A deceitful and manipulative suitor.

Significance: He pretends to be reasonable but is as corrupt as Antinous. He attempts to negotiate when Odysseus attacks.

Laertes

Role: Odysseus’ aging father.

Significance: Laertes represents the older generation and regains his strength upon Odysseus’ return.

Tiresias

Role: Blind prophet of the Underworld.

Significance: Tiresias warns Odysseus about future challenges, guiding him on how to appease Poseidon.

Nausicaa

Role: Princess of the Phaeacians.

Significance: She aids Odysseus and embodies youthful hospitality and innocence.

Alcinous

Role: King of the Phaeacians.

Significance: He provides Odysseus with a ship to return home, fulfilling the final part of his journey.

Arete

Role: Queen of the Phaeacians.

Significance: A wise and influential figure, Arete helps Odysseus gain her husband's favor.

The Sirens

Role: Mythical creatures who lure sailors to their doom with their song.

Significance: They represent temptation and the power of knowledge that can be dangerous.

Scylla and Charybdis

Role: Monsters that Odysseus must navigate between.

Significance: They symbolize unavoidable danger and the necessity of making difficult choices.
