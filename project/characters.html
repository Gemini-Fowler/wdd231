The Odyssey: Character Profiles

Odysseus

Role: Protagonist, King of Ithaca, and a hero of the Trojan War.

Significance: Odysseus is known for his intelligence, cunning, and resilience. His decade-long journey home tests his endurance and leadership. He embodies the Greek ideal of metis (cunning intelligence) and is favored by Athena but opposed by Poseidon.

Penelope

Role: Queen of Ithaca, wife of Odysseus, and mother of Telemachus.

Significance: Penelope is a symbol of loyalty, patience, and cleverness. She fends off the suitors by weaving and unweaving Laertes’ shroud, all the while waiting for Odysseus' return. She even tests him with the “bed trick” to confirm his identity.

Telemachus

Role: Son of Odysseus and Penelope.

Significance: His journey to find his father is a coming-of-age story where he grows from an inexperienced youth into a capable leader. He plays a crucial role in helping Odysseus reclaim Ithaca.

Athena

Role: Goddess of wisdom and war, protector of Odysseus.

Significance: Athena actively guides both Odysseus and Telemachus. Often disguising herself, she ensures Odysseus’ success and the eventual restoration of order in Ithaca.

Poseidon

Role: God of the sea, antagonist to Odysseus.

Significance: Poseidon seeks revenge on Odysseus for blinding his son, Polyphemus. His relentless pursuit creates many of the obstacles in Odysseus’ journey home.

Polyphemus

Role: Cyclops and son of Poseidon.

Significance: Odysseus blinds Polyphemus to escape captivity, a clever act that greatly angers Poseidon and sets the stage for later retribution.

Calypso

Role: Nymph who detains Odysseus on her island, Ogygia.

Significance: Although she offers Odysseus immortality, he yearns for home. Her role underscores themes of temptation and the human need for belonging.

Circe

Role: Enchantress who turns Odysseus’ men into pigs.

Significance: Representing both temptation and transformation, Circe eventually aids Odysseus once he resists her magic, highlighting a complex interplay of peril and assistance.

Eumaeus

Role: Loyal swineherd of Odysseus.

Significance: Eumaeus embodies loyalty and the traditional Greek value of xenia (hospitality), playing a key role in the eventual reclamation of Ithaca.

Antinous

Role: The most arrogant and aggressive of the suitors.

Significance: As the leading suitor, his actions contribute to the chaos in Odysseus’ home, making him one of the first targets of retribution.

Eurymachus

Role: A deceitful and manipulative suitor.

Significance: His false sense of reason masks his corruption. He even attempts to negotiate when Odysseus begins his revenge, highlighting the disintegration of order among the suitors.

Laertes

Role: The aging father of Odysseus.

Significance: Laertes represents the older generation and is notably restored to strength upon Odysseus' return, symbolizing the renewal of family and order.

Tiresias

Role: Blind prophet of the Underworld.

Significance: By warning Odysseus about future challenges and how to appease Poseidon, Tiresias plays a crucial role in guiding the hero’s actions.

Nausicaa

Role: Princess of the Phaeacians.

Significance: With her kindness and youthful grace, she assists Odysseus when he washes ashore, setting in motion events that lead to his eventual homecoming.

Alcinous

Role: King of the Phaeacians.

Significance: His hospitality and support enable Odysseus to finally return home, marking the pivotal conclusion to his arduous journey.

Arete

Role: Queen of the Phaeacians.

Significance: A wise and influential figure at the Phaeacian court, Arete plays a crucial role in ensuring Odysseus’ successful reintegration upon his return to Ithaca.

The Sirens

Role: Mythical beings whose songs lure sailors to doom.

Significance: They represent the seductive power of temptation, making their presence a crucial test of will and leadership.

Scylla and Charybdis

Role: The monstrous hazards Odysseus must confront.

Significance: These figures symbolize unavoidable danger and the often painful choices one must make in the face of overwhelming odds.
